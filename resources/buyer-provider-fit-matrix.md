# Buyer-Provider Fit Matrix

This is a quick reference for matching workflow types to best-fit buyer situations.

| Workflow type | Best-fit buyer | Typical trigger | What good proof looks like |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| SEO operations | growth team, content team, founder-led company | inconsistent publishing, slow briefs, messy refresh cycles | content workflow demo, CMS handoff, sample output set |
| Customer support | support lead, operations lead, SaaS team | backlog, repetitive ticket classes, uneven response quality | escalation map, QA logic, response workflow examples |
| Sales and RevOps | revenue leader, founder, sales ops | poor lead routing, messy CRM hygiene, weak follow-up discipline | routing logic, CRM workflow screenshots, review boundaries |
| Research and analysis | founder, strategy lead, product lead | too much manual synthesis, recurring market scans, slow competitor tracking | repeatable report outputs, source handling rules, example briefs |
| Internal operations | ops lead, finance lead, admin-heavy team | repetitive back-office work, reporting drag, process fragmentation | automation flow, approvals path, exception handling |
| QA and reporting | agency owner, delivery lead, operations manager | inconsistent QA, missed checks, reporting overhead | checklist logic, sample reports, alerting or review examples |

## What Usually Breaks Fit

- the provider only shows model capability, not workflow ownership
- the buyer need is too vague to scope
- nobody has defined review responsibility
- the claimed workflow spans too many departments at once
