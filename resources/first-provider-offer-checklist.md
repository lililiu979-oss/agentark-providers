# First Provider Offer Checklist

Your first offer should feel easy to trust, easy to scope, and easy to buy.

## Good First Offer Characteristics

- one workflow
- one buyer type
- one clear outcome
- one visible review boundary
- one proof set

## Avoid These Early Mistakes

- trying to cover too many workflows at once
- promising full autonomy without review boundaries
- selling “an AI employee for everything”
- hiding proof behind vague claims
- making the first offer too custom to compare or price

## Pressure Test

Can a buyer answer these quickly?

- What exactly does this agent help with?
- What input is required?
- What output do I receive?
- What still needs human approval?
- What would a pilot look like?
- Why should I trust this provider?

## Strong First Offer Patterns

- SEO refresh workflow for content teams
- support triage workflow for SaaS support teams
- CRM cleanup and routing workflow for RevOps teams
- recurring research brief workflow for product or strategy teams
- internal reporting or QA workflow for ops-heavy teams

## Before You Publish Or Apply

- tighten the offer name
- narrow the scope
- attach proof
- define escalation logic
- make the first engagement commercially legible

If your first offer sounds smaller but more believable, that is usually a good sign.
